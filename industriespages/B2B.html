About Business-to-Business (B2B) Commerce

Level 1 credit cards, generally used in business-to-consumer commerce, require the least amount of data to be included within a transaction. .

Purchase cards, referred to as Level 3 cards, are utilized by government agencies and large corporations. To qualify for the lowest processing rate, Level 3 transactions necessitate the submission of more than twenty data elements.

Corporate cards, known as Level 2 credit cards, are commonly utilized for business expenses. While Level 2 transactions generally do not have specific processing requirements, card-issuing companies can impose spending limits and define allowable goods. To secure the lowest rates, additional information may be required for certain transactions.

Incomplete data elements lead to downgrades (additional fees) for Level 2 and Level 3 cards. Understanding the necessary elements for the lowest processing rate is crucial. Igpay's payment gateway seamlessly adds required data, preventing downgrades and reducing interchange rates for Level 2 and Level 3 transactions.

Business and consumer payment processing differ in complexity and scale.

Distinguishing Business Payment Processing from Consumer Payment Processing: Business payment processing caters to the unique needs of enterprises, involving intricate transactions, often in bulk, with tailored solutions for B2B commerce. Unlike consumer payment processing, it requires handling corporate credit cards, Level 2 and Level 3 transactions, and comprehensive data elements to secure lower interchange rates. Understanding these distinctions is crucial for optimal payment solutions.

Credit card processing operates on a three-level system known as Level 1, Level 2, and Level 3. Each level involves varying data requirements, with Level 3 requiring the most detailed information for optimal transaction rates.
